What Is CRUX?

Faith Formation that takes
the faith seriously.

CRUX is a Catholic faith formation platform built for students, families, teachers, and seekers. It combines structured learning, interactive challenges, Bible study, and adult formation content — all grounded in authentic Catholic teaching and designed for the way people actually learn today.

This is the alpha — the first version, shared with a small circle of people who want to help shape what CRUX becomes. Your feedback, your engagement, and your honest critique will build the platform for everyone who comes after you.

Who Is It For?

Everyone on the journey.

✟
Students (Grades 3–8)

Faith formation modules, Bible studies, games that earn XP, challenges, and saint trading cards. Learning that doesn't feel like homework.

🏫
Teachers & Catechists

A full teacher dashboard with roster management, lesson plans, discussion guides, and the ability to award XP to your students.

🔍
Seekers & Adults

An honest, intelligent adult formation track for people curious about Catholicism, returning after time away, or deepening a faith they already have.

👨‍👩‍👧
Families

Resources that connect what children learn in class to conversations at home — because faith formation doesn't end at the parish door.

What's Inside

Everything in one place.

01
Faith Formation Modules

Structured lessons on the Trinity, Sacraments, Jesus, Mary, and conscience — for youth and adults alike.

02
Bible Study Resources

Studies for every age, from the Psalms and Gospel stories for young students to Romans and John for adults.

03
Adult Formation Track

A 7-module journey through the Catholic faith designed for seekers and returning Catholics. Honest, deep, welcoming.

04
Gamification & XP

Students earn XP, unlock saint trading cards, complete challenges, and climb the class leaderboard.

05
Teacher Dashboard

Roster management, lesson plans with timestamped discussion guides, and direct XP awards for students.

06
Practice Games

Six game types — flashcards, word scramble, matching pairs, true/false, fill-in-the-blank, and drag & drop — across all modules.
