This week we're in Romans 1:16–17, which is really the thesis statement for the whole letter. Before we get into the word study, I want to start with a simple question: when you hear the word "righteous," what does it make you think of? Don't look anything up — just first instinct.

Honestly my first thought was "holier than thou"
Same. I think "righteous" has been kind of ruined as a word. It sounds like someone who never does anything wrong and is smug about it. But in the module it talked about "dikaiosyne" meaning more like "being in right standing" which is completely different.
MC
Mrs. Carroll Teacher 10:02 AM
Marcus exactly right — the word "righteous" in English carries a lot of baggage that the Greek doesn't. Dikaiosynē is a legal and relational term. Think of it less as "morally perfect" and more as "acquitted" or "restored to right relationship." A defendant who is declared innocent is dikaiōtheis — justified, put back in right standing. That framing changes everything about what Paul is saying.
