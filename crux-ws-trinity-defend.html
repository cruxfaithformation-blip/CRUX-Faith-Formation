Defend the Faith
Use scripture and the Creed to respond to three challenges
A friend, a classmate, or someone online might challenge your faith. These exercises help you think through a real response — not just "because the Church says so," but with reasons.

Tools for Your Response

  • Nicene Creed — "I believe in one Lord Jesus Christ, the Only Begotten Son of God, born of the Father before all ages. God from God, Light from Light, true God from true God, begotten, not made, consubstantial with the Father."
  • Matthew 28:19 — "Go and baptize them in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit."
  • 1 John 4:8 — "God is love."
  • John 1:1 — "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God."
  • CCC 253 — "The Trinity is One. We do not confess three Gods, but one God in three persons."
Challenges
Challenge 1
"Christians say they believe in one God, but then they pray to the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. That's three gods — not one."
My response:
Helpful hint: See CCC 253 ("one God in three persons") and Matthew 28:19 (note that Jesus says "name" — singular — not "names").
Challenge 2
"Jesus was a great teacher and prophet, but calling him God is going too far. Even he prayed to God — so he can't BE God."
My response:
Helpful hint: See the Nicene Creed ("true God from true God, begotten, not made") and John 1:1 ("the Word was God").
Challenge 3
"The word 'Trinity' doesn't appear anywhere in the Bible, so it's just something the Church made up centuries later."
My response:
Helpful hint: See Matthew 28:19 (Father, Son, and Spirit named together), 1 John 4:8 (if God is love, he must have someone to love eternally — that requires inner relationship), and note: the concept being biblical is what matters, not whether the exact word appears.
Reflection
Which challenge was hardest to answer? Why?
