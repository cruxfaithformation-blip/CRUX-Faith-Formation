A Mother's Prayer
Lord Jesus, you stopped everything to hold children — when your disciples thought there were more important things to attend to. Remind us that there is nothing more important.
Watch over every child who is afraid, every child who is lonely, every child who needs to know they are loved. Let them come to you. Do not let us hinder them.
And make us humble enough, trusting enough, to enter the kingdom you promised to those who are like them. Amen.
This moment in Matthew 19 is one of the most quietly revolutionary scenes in the Gospels. People were bringing their children to Jesus to have him bless them. The disciples tried to turn them away — they had more important things to attend to.
Jesus rebuked them. Not gently. And then he said what has echoed for two thousand years: “Let the little children come to me.” He placed his hands on them. He prayed over them. He gave them the time his disciples had decided they didn't deserve.
The Church has returned to this scene again and again — in every argument about who belongs in the family of God. Jesus answered simply, by stopping and kneeling down.
