Section 2

Adding and reordering slides

Build your lesson in any order — you can always rearrange later.

1
Add a new slide
Click the + Add Slide button at the bottom of the slide list. A new blank content slide will appear. You can then change its type in the edit panel.
2
Change the slide type
In the controls strip below the slide, click any type in the type grid. The slide canvas updates immediately to show the new layout.
3
Reorder by dragging
Click and hold any slide thumbnail in the left panel, then drag it to a new position. A gold line shows where it will land. Release to drop.
Tip: You can also use the small ▲ ▼ arrows that appear when you hover over a slide thumbnail.
4
Delete a slide
Select the slide, then click the Delete button in the top header bar. It appears automatically when a slide is selected and more than one slide exists. You'll be asked to confirm before it's removed.

Section 4

Formatting your content

You edit directly on the slide. Click any text region and type. A small toolbar in the top-right corner of the slide lets you style individual lines.

—
Bullet (— Bullet)
Click a line in the body area, then click — Bullet in the toolbar. A gold dash (—) appears before the line. Use for lists of key points.
★
Gold highlight (★ Gold)
Click a line, then click ★ Gold in the toolbar. The line turns gold with a star prefix — the visual emphasis of the slide. Use for the single most important point.
❝
Quote block (❝ Quote)
Click a line, then click ❝ Quote. The line becomes an italicized quote with a gold left border — good for short scripture excerpts or memorable statements inside a content slide.
¶
Paragraph (¶ Para)
Plain body text. No bullet, no prefix. Good for continuous prose rather than a list. The default style for new lines.
↵
Adding and removing lines
Press Enter to create a new line of the same type below the current one. Press Backspace on an empty line to delete it. You can also use the + Line and ✕ buttons in the toolbar.
📝
Speaker notes
The Presenter Notes field lives in the controls strip below the slide. Notes never appear on the projected slide — only when you press N during presentation. Use them for reminders, timing cues, or discussion prompts.
💾
Saving
Your presentation saves automatically as you type. There is no save button. If you close and reopen the page, your slides will still be there. Use the Reset button in the header to restore the original CRUX Trinity lesson.
Section 5

Vocabulary toggle

Each slide can carry two versions of its content — one for older students and one for younger children. The Vocab toggle in the header switches between them instantly, even during a live presentation.

1
Advanced mode
The default. Uses full theological vocabulary — Trinity, Incarnation, Hypostasis, Nicene Creed. Appropriate for middle school, high school, and adult groups.
2
Child-Friendly mode
Switches to simpler language written for elementary-age children. The content is the same truth, just in words a 7-year-old can understand.
How to write both versions: The Advanced / Child-Friendly toggle in the controls strip below the slide switches which version you're currently editing. Switch to Child-Friendly, then edit the slide text to write the simpler version. If you only write one version, both modes show the same content.
3
Switch during class
The toggle is available both in the editor and in the live presentation view. You can switch mid-lesson without interrupting the flow — useful if you teach multiple age groups back to back.

💡
Projecting to a screen
Open the presentation on the computer connected to your projector or TV. Click Present, then use your keyboard or the on-screen arrows to advance slides. The dot bar at the bottom shows your position in the deck.
💡
Speaker notes during class
Press N during the presentation to show your speaker notes at the bottom of the screen. These are visible to you but are not projected — they appear as a translucent overlay only on your device.
💡
Switching vocabulary mid-lesson
The Vocab toggle is available in the bottom control bar during presentation. Switch between Advanced and Child-Friendly at any time without leaving the presentation.
